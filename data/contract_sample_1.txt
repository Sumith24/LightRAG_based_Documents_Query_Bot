SECTION 1: DEFINITIONS
In this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:
 Aircraft: The Boeing Voyager -737, having the manufacturer's serial number 1013398 and 
registration number N67890, as described in Exhibit A.
 Basic Rent: The amount payable by the Lessee for the lease of the Aircraft, calculated as 
$250,000 per month, as detailed in Section 4.
 Delivery Date: The date on which the Aircraft is delivered to and accepted by the Lessee, as 
described in Section 3.
 Lease Term: The term during which the Aircraft is leased to the Lessee, as specified in 
Section 3.

precedent set forth in Section 3.

SECTION 6: POSSESSION AND USE
1. Possession: The Lessee shall take possession of the Aircraft on the Delivery Date.
2. Use: The Lessee agrees to use the Aircraft solely for commercial passenger transport and

3. Operational Control: The Lessee shall maintain full operational control over the Aircraft

3. Reporting: The Lessee shall provide the Lessor with periodic reports regarding the Aircraft's

SECTION 8: COVENANTS OF LESSEE
Compliance with Laws:
The Lessee shall, at all times during the Lease Term, comply with all applicable local, national, and 
international laws, rules, treaties, and regulations governing the operation, use, registration, and 
maintenance of the Aircraft, including but not limited to those established by the Federal Aviation 
Administration (FAA), the European Union Aviation Safety Agency (EASA), and the International Civil 
Aviation Organization (ICAO).
The Lessee shall obtain and maintain all licenses, permits, and certifications required for the 
operation of the Aircraft, including air operator certificates, noise certifications, and environmental 
permits.
The Lessee shall ensure that the Aircraft is operated only by duly licensed and qualified pilots and 
crew members, as required by applicable aviation authorities, and in accordance with the Aircraft's 
operating manuals and guidelines.
In the event of any regulatory change that affects the operation or maintenance of the Aircraft, the 
Lessee agrees to promptly make any necessary modifications or changes to comply with such laws or 
regulations at its own expense.
Maintenance Obligations:
The Lessee shall ensure that the Aircraft is maintained in an airworthy condition, consistent with the 
standards required by the manufacturer and all applicable aviation authorities. The Lessee shall 
perform all routine and scheduled maintenance, repairs, and inspections, including but not limited 
to:
A-checks, B-checks, C-checks, and D-checks as specified in the Aircraft’s maintenance schedule and in 
accordance with the manufacturer’s maintenance manual;
Engine inspections, including borescope inspections, and overhauls as required by the engine 
manufacturer’s maintenance program;
Landing gear inspections, refurbishments, or replacements as specified by the manufacturer or 
regulatory authorities.
The Lessee shall ensure that all repairs are carried out by certified and approved maintenance 
organizations, and that the Aircraft is operated in accordance with any Airworthiness Directives 
(ADs), Service Bulletins (SBs), and Mandatory Modifications issued by the manufacturer or aviation 
authorities.
The Lessee agrees to maintain accurate and up-to-date maintenance records, logs, and technical 
documents for the Aircraft, and to make such records available to the Lessor for inspection upon 
request. Such records shall include:
Flight logs, detailing all flight hours and cycles;
Maintenance logs, documenting all repairs, inspections, and modifications made to the Aircraft;
9
Component records, detailing the status and life-cycle of key components such as engines, landing 
gear, and avionics.
The Lessee shall bear all costs associated with maintaining, repairing, and operating the Aircraft, 
including but not limited to labor, parts, materials, and regulatory fees.
Operational Insurance:
The Lessee shall, at its own cost, obtain and maintain in full force and effect during the entire Lease 
Term, comprehensive aviation insurance coverage, including but not limited to the following types of 
coverage:
Hull All-Risk Insurance: Covering the full replacement value of the Aircraft against physical loss or 
damage resulting from any cause, including accidents, fire, theft, vandalism, and natural disasters;
Hull War Risk Insurance: Covering damage or loss resulting from acts of war, terrorism, hijacking, or 
other hostile acts, as well as confiscation or seizure by government authorities;
Third-Party Liability Insurance: Providing coverage for bodily injury, death, and property damage to 
third parties, with minimum limits of liability of $500 million per occurrence, or such higher amounts 
as may be required by applicable law or the Lessor;
Passenger Liability Insurance: Covering the legal liability of the Lessee for bodily injury or death of 
passengers, with a minimum coverage of $100,000 per passenger or as required by applicable law.
All insurance policies must:
Name the Lessor as an additional insured and loss payee;
Be issued by reputable insurance carriers with an A.M. Best rating of at least A- or equivalent;
Include a provision requiring 30 days' prior written notice to the Lessor before any cancellation, 
material modification, or expiration of the policy;
Include a waiver of subrogation in favour of the Lessor.
The Lessee agrees to provide the Lessor with certificates of insurance evidencing compliance with 
this Section, and to deliver such certificates on or before the commencement of the Lease Term, and 
upon each renewal or replacement of the policies.
In the event of a claim, the Lessee shall immediately notify the Lessor and cooperate fully with the 
Lessor and the insurer in handling the claim, including providing all necessary documentation, access 
to the Aircraft, and participation in any investigations.
Operational Control and Use of the Aircraft:
10
The Lessee shall maintain full operational control of the Aircraft during the Lease Term and agrees 
that the Aircraft will only be operated for lawful purposes, and in accordance with:
The manufacturer’s operating manual;
The approved operating limits and flight performance standards;
The specific purposes outlined in the lease, such as commercial passenger transport or cargo 
operations.
The Lessee agrees not to use the Aircraft for any illegal purposes, including but not limited to:
Transporting unlawful goods or materials (e.g., contraband, weapons, etc.);
Conducting unauthorized flights or operating in prohibited airspace;
Engaging in illegal smuggling or trafficking activities.
The Aircraft shall be based at Houston International Airport, unless otherwise agreed in writing by 
the Lessor. The Lessee shall notify the Lessor in writing before moving the Aircraft to any other 
jurisdiction or country.
Modifications and Alterations:
The Lessee shall not make any material modifications, alterations, or improvements to the Aircraft 
without the prior written consent of the Lessor, except for routine repairs and replacements that are 
required for the Aircraft to remain airworthy and compliant with applicable regulations.
Any approved modifications or upgrades to the Aircraft, including changes to the avionics, interior, or 
exterior, must be performed by a certified maintenance organization and comply with all regulatory 
requirements. The Lessee agrees to bear the cost of such modifications.
Upon termination of the Lease, the Lessee agrees to restore the Aircraft to its original configuration, 
unless otherwise agreed in writing by the Lessor.
Operational Reporting:
The Lessee shall provide the Lessor with regular operational reports during the Lease Term, 
including:
Monthly flight reports, detailing flight hours, cycles, and any incidents or maintenance issues;
Quarterly maintenance reports, summarizing all maintenance activities and inspections performed 
on the Aircraft;
Annual financial statements, detailing any operational costs, including maintenance, fuel, crew 
salaries, and other expenses incurred in operating the Aircraft.
11
The Lessee shall promptly notify the Lessor of any accidents, incidents, or mechanical failures 
involving the Aircraft and shall submit a written report detailing the nature and cause of the event, 
and the steps taken to rectify the situation.
Taxes and Fees:
The Lessee shall be solely responsible for paying all taxes, duties, fees, and other charges imposed by 
any governmental authority in connection with the possession, use, or operation of the Aircraft, 
including but not limited to:
Sales tax, use tax, and excise tax on the Lease payments;
Airport landing fees, navigation charges, and airport parking fees;
Any customs duties or import/export fees in the event that the Aircraft is operated across 
international borders.
The Lessee agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the Lessor from any tax liabilities or penalties 
arising from the Lessee’s failure to comply with its tax obligations.
Environmental and Safety Compliance:
The Lessee agrees to comply with all applicable environmental laws and regulations related to the 
operation of the Aircraft, including noise abatement procedures, emissions standards, and 
environmental impact assessments.
The Lessee shall not use or store any hazardous materials on board the Aircraft unless such materials 
are necessary for the operation of the Aircraft and are handled in accordance with applicable safety 
regulations.
The Lessee shall ensure that all necessary safety equipment is on board the Aircraft and in good 
working order, including but not limited to fire extinguishers, oxygen systems, life rafts, and 
emergency medical kits.

SECTION 11: CASUALTY OCCURRENCES
Definition of Casualty:
A "Casualty Occurrence" shall refer to any event that causes significant physical damage to the 
Aircraft, rendering it unfit for safe or legal operation, or results in the total destruction or irreparable 
loss of the Aircraft. Such events may include, but are not limited to:
Accidents: Any event where the Aircraft is involved in a collision or other form of impact, either inflight or on the ground, resulting in structural or mechanical damage.
Hijacking or Unlawful Seizure: Any situation where the Aircraft is unlawfully taken or commandeered 
by individuals or groups, resulting in damage or operational impairment.
Fire, Explosion, or Natural Disaster: Events such as fire, explosion, flooding, or any other natural 
disaster that causes material damage to the Aircraft.
Acts of Terrorism or Sabotage: Any malicious act intended to cause damage to or destruction of the 
Aircraft, including acts of terrorism, sabotage, or other criminal activity.
War or Hostile Acts: Any damage resulting from war, military action, or other hostile engagements, 
including but not limited to the Aircraft being shot down, bombed, or damaged by other forms of 
combat.
Lessee’s Obligations in the Event of a Casualty:
In the event of a Casualty Occurrence, the Lessee shall promptly undertake the following actions:
Immediate Notification: The Lessee shall notify the Lessor in writing within 24 hours of the 
occurrence, providing an initial report with the details of the event, the extent of the damage, and 
any potential risks to the Aircraft. The Lessee must provide a comprehensive written report within 
five business days of the occurrence, including all relevant facts, witness statements, technical 
evaluations, and initial assessments of the damage.
Prevention of Further Damage: The Lessee must take all reasonable steps necessary to protect the 
Aircraft from further damage or deterioration following the Casualty Occurrence. This includes 
securing the Aircraft, preventing unauthorized access, and arranging for the Aircraft to be 
transported to a safe location, if necessary.
Collaboration with Authorities: If the Casualty Occurrence involves criminal activity (e.g., hijacking, 
terrorism) or regulatory breaches, the Lessee shall fully cooperate with law enforcement, aviation 
authorities, and other relevant agencies. The Lessee shall also ensure that the Lessor is kept 
informed of all communications with such authorities.
Repair or Salvage Assessments: The Lessee shall arrange for immediate assessments of the damage 
by certified technicians, surveyors, or aviation engineers to determine whether the Aircraft can be 
repaired or salvaged. A copy of all assessments, along with detailed repair or salvage plans, must be 
submitted to the Lessor for review and approval.
Temporary Replacement: If the Aircraft is temporarily unfit for use but not a total loss, the Lessee 
may, at its own cost, provide a temporary replacement aircraft (subject to the Lessor’s approval) until 
the Aircraft is repaired or returned to service.
15
Insurance Proceeds in Case of Total Loss:
If the Aircraft is deemed a Total Loss, meaning it is destroyed, rendered permanently unfit for use, or 
the cost of repair exceeds the market value of the Aircraft, the following provisions shall apply:
Total Loss Declaration: The Aircraft shall be deemed a Total Loss if:
The Aircraft is destroyed or damaged beyond economical repair;
It is permanently lost or cannot be recovered (e.g., due to hijacking, unlawful seizure, or 
disappearance);
It has been condemned as unfit for service by an aviation authority or other relevant regulatory 
body.
Assignment of Insurance Proceeds: In the event of a Total Loss, the Lessee shall assign all rights to 
the insurance proceeds to the Lessor, as specified in Section 12 (Insurance). The insurance proceeds 
shall include any amounts payable under the Hull All-Risk Insurance, War Risk Insurance, and other 
relevant policies. The Lessee shall:
Notify the insurance provider immediately of the Total Loss and ensure that all claims are submitted 
and processed in a timely manner;
Cooperate fully with the Lessor and the insurers to facilitate the prompt settlement of claims;
Assign all claims, payments, and recovery rights to the Lessor.
Use of Proceeds: The Lessor shall have sole discretion over the use of the insurance proceeds, which 
may include:
Applying the proceeds to settle any outstanding amounts owed by the Lessee under this Agreement;
Utilizing the funds to repair or replace the Aircraft, if feasible;
Retaining the proceeds in the event of termination of the Lease.
Termination or Replacement in Case of Total Loss:
Upon a Casualty Occurrence resulting in a Total Loss, the following options may be exercised:
Termination of the Agreement:
The Lessor shall have the right to terminate this Agreement upon written notice to the Lessee in the 
event of a Total Loss. Upon termination, the Lessee shall promptly return any records, documents, or 
parts related to the Aircraft that were not destroyed in the Casualty Occurrence. The Lessee shall 
also pay any outstanding amounts owed under this Agreement, including Rent, Additional Rent, or 
other fees accrued up to the date of termination.
Any remaining insurance proceeds, after settlement of the Lessee’s obligations, shall be retained by 
the Lessor.
Replacement of the Aircraft (Subject to Lessor’s Consent):
16
In lieu of termination, the Lessee may propose the replacement of the Aircraft with a similar aircraft 
of comparable value, age, and condition, subject to the Lessor’s prior written approval. The 
replacement aircraft must meet the following criteria:
It must be of the same make, model, and configuration, or a model approved by the Lessor;
It must be in good working order, airworthy, and compliant with all applicable aviation regulations;
The replacement must be delivered to the Lessor within 90 days of the Casualty Occurrence, unless 
otherwise agreed in writing.
All costs associated with the procurement, delivery, and registration of the replacement aircraft shall 
be borne by the Lessee.
If the Lessor accepts the replacement aircraft, the terms of this Agreement shall continue, with the 
replacement aircraft being subject to all the same terms and conditions applicable to the original 
Aircraft.
Restoration of the Aircraft:
In some cases where the Aircraft is not deemed a Total Loss, but requires significant repair, the 
Lessee may elect to restore the Aircraft to its original condition, subject to the Lessor’s written 
approval of the repair plan. All costs of the restoration shall be borne by the Lessee.
Upon completion of repairs, the Aircraft shall undergo a thorough inspection and certification 
process by a certified aviation authority before being returned to service.
Partial Damage and Repairs:
In the event the Aircraft sustains damage but is not considered a Total Loss, the Lessee shall, at its 
own expense, arrange for the Aircraft to be promptly repaired and restored to the condition required 
under this Agreement. Repairs must:
Be carried out by a certified and approved maintenance organization, in accordance with the 
manufacturer’s guidelines and all applicable regulatory requirements;
Be completed within a reasonable time frame, as agreed upon by the Lessor and Lessee.
During the repair period, the Lessee shall continue to pay Rent, Additional Rent, and all other fees 
due under this Agreement, unless otherwise agreed by the Lessor.

SECTION 12: INSURANCE
1. Types of Insurance:
The Lessee agrees to maintain, at its own expense, the following types of insurance for the 
Aircraft throughout the Lease Term. The insurance policies must be procured from insurance 
companies with an A.M. Best rating of at least A- or equivalent, and must provide coverage 
that is consistent with industry standards for commercial aircraft operations.
o Hull All-Risk Insurance:
 Coverage must be equal to or exceed the full replacement value of the 
Aircraft. This coverage shall protect against physical loss or damage to the 
Aircraft arising from all risks of flight and ground operation, including but not 
limited to accidents, fire, theft, vandalism, lightning, windstorm, and 
collision.
 The policy must include provisions for partial damage and total loss of the 
Aircraft, with any claims proceeds payable to the Lessor as the loss payee.
o Third-Party Liability Insurance:
 The Lessee shall maintain third-party liability insurance with coverage limits 
of no less than $500 million per occurrence. This coverage must protect 
against legal liabilities arising from bodily injury, death, or property damage 
caused to third parties in connection with the operation or use of the 
Aircraft.
 The policy must include coverage for liabilities arising from ground handling 
operations, passenger-related claims, and damage caused to airport 
property or other aircraft.
o Passenger Liability Insurance:
 The Lessee must maintain passenger liability coverage that provides for 
bodily injury or death to passengers aboard the Aircraft, with a minimum 
coverage of $100,000 per passenger, or as required by applicable law or 
regulations.
 This coverage must extend to all operations, including domestic and 
international flights, and apply regardless of whether the claim arises from 
an accident, incident, or other operational event.
o War Risk Insurance:
 The Lessee shall maintain war risk insurance to cover losses arising from war, 
terrorism, hijacking, sabotage, unlawful seizure of the Aircraft, and other 
war-related perils.
 Coverage should include confiscation, seizure, or nationalization of the 
Aircraft by government or military forces, and the policy limits must be no 
less than $100 million or as specified by the Lessor.
 The policy should also cover civil commotions, riots, and other violent 
disturbances that may affect the Aircraft.
18
o Crew Liability Insurance:
 The Lessee shall maintain insurance to cover any liability arising from claims 
made by or on behalf of crew members of the Aircraft in relation to bodily 
injury, death, or other personal harm. The minimum coverage shall be $50 
million per occurrence or as required by local labor laws and international 
regulations.
o Cargo and Baggage Liability Insurance:
 If the Aircraft is used for cargo operations, the Lessee must maintain cargo 
liability insurance covering any loss, damage, or destruction of cargo 
transported on the Aircraft.
 The policy must provide for coverage limits consistent with the value of the 
cargo carried and comply with any applicable laws governing cargo liability.
 Coverage must also extend to personal baggage of passengers, with 
minimum coverage limits of $1,500 per passenger.
o Ground Risk Insurance:
 The Lessee must obtain ground risk insurance to cover the Aircraft when it is 
not in motion (hangar, storage, etc.), including protection against theft, fire, 
and natural disasters.
 The policy must provide coverage for full replacement value or repair costs if 
the Aircraft is damaged while grounded.
2. Insurance Policies:
All insurance policies required under this Agreement must include the following provisions:
o Lessor as Additional Insured and Loss Payee:
 The Lessor shall be named as an additional insured and loss payee under all 
insurance policies, ensuring that the Lessor is fully protected in the event of 
a claim or loss. Any claims proceeds under the Hull All-Risk, War Risk, or 
Third-Party Liability policies shall be payable directly to the Lessor, or to the 
Lessee and Lessor jointly.
 The policies shall specify that the Lessor’s rights and interests will not be 
invalidated or diminished by any acts, omissions, or negligence on the part 
of the Lessee or its agents.
o Non-Cancellation Clause:
 Each insurance policy must include a 30-day prior written notice provision 
requiring the insurer to notify the Lessor in writing at least 30 days before 
any cancellation, non-renewal, or material change in the terms or coverage 
of the policy.
 In cases where war risk coverage is reduced or cancelled due to an increase 
in hostilities or political unrest, the insurer must provide seven days’ notice 
to the Lessor, with the Lessee taking immediate steps to replace or restore 
the required coverage.
19
o Waiver of Subrogation:
 All policies must include a waiver of subrogation in favor of the Lessor, 
ensuring that the insurers cannot seek reimbursement from the Lessor for 
any claims paid under the policy, even in cases where the Lessor is found to 
have contributed to the cause of loss or damage.
o Primary Insurance:
 The policies must be primary and non-contributory, meaning they take 
precedence over any other insurance policies held by the Lessor or its 
affiliates. The policies shall cover the full amount of any claim without 
reduction by other insurance the Lessor may carry.
3. Proof of Insurance:
The Lessee agrees to provide the Lessor with certificates of insurance and any other 
documents or evidence of insurance coverage as required by the Lessor. This must be done 
prior to the Delivery Date of the Aircraft and upon any renewal, replacement, or modification 
of the policies. Such proof must include:
o Certificates of Insurance:
 The Lessee shall deliver certificates of insurance issued by the insurance 
companies, confirming the types and amounts of coverage, naming the 
Lessor as additional insured and loss payee, and confirming compliance with 
all other requirements outlined in this Agreement.
o Policy Endorsements:
 The Lessee shall provide copies of all endorsements, riders, and 
amendments to the policies that may affect the Lessor’s rights or coverage. 
This includes any modifications to coverage limits, cancellation clauses, and 
named insureds.
o Renewal Certificates:
 At least 30 days before the expiration of any insurance policy, the Lessee 
shall provide the Lessor with renewal certificates or proof of replacement 
coverage ensuring no lapse in insurance protection.
o Notice of Claims:
 In the event of any loss, damage, or occurrence giving rise to a claim under 
the insurance policies, the Lessee shall immediately notify the Lessor and 
the insurers in writing, detailing the nature and extent of the claim. The 
Lessee shall provide the Lessor with all information and documentation 
required to process the claim and assist in pursuing any recovery from the 
insurers.
4. Failure to Maintain Insurance:
o If the Lessee fails to procure or maintain any of the required insurance coverage, the 
Lessor may, at its option:
20
 Procure such insurance on behalf of the Lessee and recover the cost from 
the Lessee as Additional Rent; or
 Declare an event of default under this Agreement and exercise any remedies 
available under Section 17.
o The Lessee shall indemnify and hold the Lessor harmless for any losses, damages, or 
liabilities incurred by the Lessor as a result of the Lessee’s failure to maintain the 
required insurance coverage.

SECTION 15: PERFECTION OF TITLE AND FURTHER ASSURANCES
• Title:
The Lessor retains full legal and beneficial title to the Aircraft during the Lease Term. The Lessee 
acknowledges that it has no right, title, or interest in the Aircraft other than the right to use it under 
the terms of this Agreement.
The Lessee agrees not to attempt or facilitate any action that would challenge or impair the Lessor's 
ownership rights, including but not limited to, any attempt to sell, transfer, pledge, mortgage, or 
otherwise encumber the Aircraft.
Any modifications, improvements, or additions made to the Aircraft by the Lessee during the Lease 
Term shall immediately vest in the Lessor, unless otherwise agreed in writing.
• Further Assurances:
The Lessee agrees to execute, acknowledge, and deliver all documents, instruments, and other forms 
of assurances as the Lessor may reasonably request to ensure and maintain the Lessor’s ownership 
rights and interest in the Aircraft.
The Lessee shall cooperate with the Lessor to ensure the Aircraft is properly registered or
